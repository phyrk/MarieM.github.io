Migrating Internal Reporting from Manual SAP Exports to SQL-Based Automated Solution

Problem:

When I joined the department, internal reporting was largely based on manual SAP data exports. Each team relied on custom Excel files with manual filters, and report-specific pivot tables. This led to:

  • Delays in report updates
  • Risk of human error in data preparation
  • Lack of consistency across reports
  • Time-consuming maintenance and low flexibility for changes

Solution

I proposed and led the transition from manual SAP data handling to a SQL-based reporting pipeline. By connecting directly to the data source via SQL and using Power Query for transformation, we created dynamic, automated reports tailored to each team’s needs.

This approach enabled:

Accurate and always up-to-date data.

Faster refreshes (no more copy-pasting).

Custom filtering and logic in Power Query.

A scalable structure for new reports and future changes

Collaboration

To ensure the new reporting structure met business requirements, I:

  • Held discovery sessions with cross-functional finance teams (payments, collection, deduction, cash flow)
  • Collected and clarified reporting needs and data logic
  • Translated business questions into SQL logic and Power Query workflows

Business Value

  • Time Saved: Reduced daily processing time significantly (exact hours depend on volume)
  • Data Accuracy: Eliminated manual errors and inconsistencies
  • Better Decisions: Real-time, clean data enabled quicker and more confident business actions
  • Scalability: New reports could be rolled out faster using the same connection and structure
